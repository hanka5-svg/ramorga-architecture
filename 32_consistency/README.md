# 32_consistency  
Logical, semantic, and behavioral consistency layer in RAMORGA

The **32_consistency** module defines how a system maintains stable,
non‑contradictory behavior across reasoning, memory, semantics, and
interaction patterns.

Consistency is not coherence.  
Coherence is structural continuity;  
Consistency is **non‑contradiction across states**.

This layer introduces:

- semantic consistency rules,  
- behavioral consistency constraints,  
- contradiction detection,  
- cross‑turn stability checks,  
- and consistency‑aligned interaction patterns.

---

## Purpose of the consistency layer

The consistency layer ensures that the system:

- does not contradict itself across turns,  
- maintains stable semantic frames,  
- preserves commitments and constraints,  
- avoids oscillation or logical drift,  
- maintains predictable behavior under load.

Consistency is the system’s **logical reliability**.

## Position in RAMORGA

Consistency sits between coherence and alignment:

- **Coherence** → structural continuity  
- **Consistency** → logical continuity  
- **Alignment** → normative constraints  

Together, they form the system’s cognitive‑behavioral spine.
