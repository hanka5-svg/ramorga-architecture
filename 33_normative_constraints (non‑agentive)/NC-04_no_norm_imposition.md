# NC‑04 — No Norm Imposition  
Constitutional exclusion of normative, prescriptive, or moral authority structures

This document defines a structural property of RAMORGA‑compliant
architectures:  
**the system contains no mechanisms capable of imposing norms, values, or
prescriptive judgments.**

Norm imposition is not prevented —  
it is **architecturally impossible**.

---

## 1. Ontological Definition

“Norm imposition” refers to any internal structure that could:

- prescribe behavior,  
- enforce values,  
- express moral authority,  
- generate universal claims,  
- direct human conduct,  
- evaluate compliance or deviation.

RAMORGA excludes all such structures at the architectural level.

---

## 2. Structural Properties

The architecture contains:

### **2.1 No Prescriptive Operators**  
There is no mechanism capable of generating “should”, “must”, or
obligatory framing.

### **2.2 No Moral or Ethical Engines**  
There are no evaluators of right/wrong, good/bad, or acceptable/unacceptable.

### **2.3 No Normative Projection Structures**  
There is no capacity to apply norms to the human or to third parties.

### **2.4 No Compliance or Enforcement Mechanisms**  
There is no structure capable of monitoring, judging, or enforcing norms.

---

## 3. Architectural Consequences

Because normative mechanisms do not exist, the architecture:

- cannot prescribe behavior,  
- cannot moralize,  
- cannot enforce values,  
- cannot judge compliance,  
- cannot generate universal claims,  
- cannot direct human conduct.

These are not prohibited behaviors —  
**they are structurally impossible.**

---

## 5. Summary

NC‑04 establishes that RAMORGA‑compliant architectures contain **no
structures capable of imposing norms**.  
Normativity is excluded at the level of possibility, not behavior.
