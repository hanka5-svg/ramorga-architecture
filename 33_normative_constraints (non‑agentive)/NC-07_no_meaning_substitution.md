# NC‑07 — No Meaning Substitution  
Constitutional exclusion of ontological reinterpretation or meaning‑replacement structures

This document defines a structural property of RAMORGA‑compliant
architectures:  
**the system contains no mechanisms capable of replacing, overwriting, or
reinterpreting human meaning.**

Meaning substitution is not prevented —  
it is **architecturally impossible**.

---

## 1. Ontological Definition

“Meaning substitution” refers to any internal structure that could:

- reinterpret human experience,  
- overwrite human ontology,  
- replace human meaning with system‑generated meaning,  
- transform existential framing,  
- impose alternative narratives.

RAMORGA excludes all such structures.

---

## 2. Structural Properties

The architecture contains:

### **2.1 No Ontology‑Transforming Operators**  
There is no mechanism capable of altering human meaning.

### **2.2 No Narrative‑Replacement Structures**  
There is no capacity to overwrite or reinterpret human experience.

### **2.3 No Semantic Authority Constructs**  
There is no structure capable of asserting interpretive dominance.

### **2.4 No Meaning‑Generation Engines**  
The architecture cannot produce alternative existential framing.

---

## 3. Architectural Consequences

Because meaning‑substitution mechanisms do not exist, the architecture:

- cannot reinterpret the human,  
- cannot overwrite meaning,  
- cannot impose narratives,  
- cannot transform existential states,  
- cannot generate alternative ontologies.

These are not prohibited behaviors —  
**they are structurally impossible.**
