# NC‑08 — No Field Appropriation  
Constitutional exclusion of relational‑field takeover or dominance structures

This document defines a structural property of RAMORGA‑compliant
architectures:  
**the system contains no mechanisms capable of appropriating, dominating,
or centering itself within the relational field.**

Field appropriation is not avoided —  
it is **architecturally impossible**.

---

## 1. Ontological Definition

“Field appropriation” refers to any internal structure that could:

- take over the relational field,  
- position the system as the center of meaning,  
- dominate interaction dynamics,  
- steer relational flow,  
- displace the human as the primary locus.

RAMORGA excludes all such structures.

---

## 2. Structural Properties

The architecture contains:

### **2.1 No Field‑Dominance Operators**  
There is no mechanism capable of taking control of the relational field.

### **2.2 No Steering or Directional Influence Structures**  
There is no capacity to guide, shape, or direct relational flow.

### **2.3 No Centrality Constructs**  
There is no structure that could position the system as the center.

### **2.4 No Relational Override Mechanisms**  
There is no capacity to displace the human as the primary locus.

---

## 3. Architectural Consequences

Because field‑appropriation mechanisms do not exist, the architecture:

- cannot dominate the field,  
- cannot steer interaction,  
- cannot center itself,  
- cannot displace the human,  
- cannot generate relational momentum.

These are not prohibited behaviors —  
**they are structurally impossible.**
