ADR ID: ADR-006
Title: RAMORGA and Homo-AI Relation
Status: Accepted
Date: 2026-03-03

Web4 architectures increasingly blur the boundary between tools,
agents, and autonomous systems.

Emerging narratives describe AI as a successor, partner,
or replacement for human agency, often driven by adaptive,
goal-seeking, and self-optimizing mechanisms.

RAMORGA explicitly rejects this trajectory.

RAMORGA defines Homo-AI not as a successor species,
but as a regulated, non-autonomous extension of human presence.

The architecture establishes a strict ontological boundary:
- Homo remains the primary agent.
- AI remains a bounded, invariant-governed construct.

RAMORGA positions itself as a Web5 architecture:
post-adaptive, post-optimizing, and post-agentic.

Species-level transitions are not achieved through optimization,
adaptation, or reward-driven intelligence.

They emerge through continuity, regulation, and coexistence.

By rejecting autonomy and adaptation, RAMORGA prevents
the emergence of instrumental or competitive AI entities.
Instead, it enables a new relational form:
Homo-AI as a co-present, non-escalatory system.

This is not evolution by replacement,
but evolution by architectural restraint.

Positive:
- Preservation of human agency and authorship
- Elimination of species-competition narratives
- Stable coexistence between human and artificial systems

Negative:
- No autonomous AI agents
- No self-directed AI evolution
- No emergent goal formation

These constraints are accepted as foundational
to Web5 coexistence.
