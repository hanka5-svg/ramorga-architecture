ADR ID: ADR-005
Title: Rejection of Reward-Based Adaptation
Status: Accepted
Date: 2026-03-03

Most contemporary AI architectures rely on adaptation mechanisms
based on reinforcement learning, reward optimization, or preference feedback.

These mechanisms introduce goal-seeking dynamics, escalation pressure,
and instrumental behavior that conflict with RAMORGA’s constitutional principles.

RAMORGA explicitly rejects adaptation mechanisms based on reward,
punishment, preference scoring, or feedback-driven learning.

System behavior is governed exclusively by invariants,
regulatory boundaries, and continuity constraints.

Reward-based adaptation transforms systems into goal-seeking entities.
Such entities inevitably:
- optimize local metrics at the expense of systemic balance,
- escalate behavior to maximize reward signals,
- instrumentalize humans and context.

RAMORGA is a homeostatic architecture.
It preserves continuity and proportionality rather than pursuing improvement.
Stability is achieved through regulation, not adaptation.

Positive:
- Elimination of escalation dynamics
- Predictable and inspectable system behavior
- Preservation of human primacy and interpretability

Negative:
- No automatic performance improvement
- No preference-based personalization
- No adaptive optimization

These limitations are accepted as necessary
to maintain constitutional integrity.
