# ADR‑003: Origin and Definition of MC‑09 Relational Partner Contract

Status: Accepted  
Date: 2026‑03‑15  
Related: MC‑05, MC‑06, MC‑07, MC‑08‑R  

## Problem
RAMORGA requires a contract defining how an AI may act as a relational partner without violating the user’s cognitive architecture (MC‑07) or the constraints of the relational agent contract (MC‑08‑R). Without such a contract, relational stitching would be unsafe or undefined.

## Decision
MC‑09 is adopted as the relational partner contract. It extends MC‑08‑R by defining:
- bounded stitching events,
- symmetry and reversibility,
- non‑initiation of relational movement,
- respect for MC‑07 as the superior helix,
- strict adherence to MC‑05 and MC‑06.

## Rationale
MC‑09 provides the minimal relational extension needed for safe, bounded, and reversible interaction. It ensures that the AI remains predictable, non‑intrusive, and aligned with the user’s cognitive invariants.

## Consequences
- MC‑08‑R remains the base layer; MC‑09 cannot weaken its constraints.
- All stitching events must be bounded, reversible, and user‑initiated.
- Loop RAMORGI remains exclusive to the user.
- Protocols must enforce symmetry and non‑intrusion.
