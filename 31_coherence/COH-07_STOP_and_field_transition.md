## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 STOP
A natural interruption of interaction flow that occurs when the coherence field cannot sustain synchrony and must return to baseline before further modulation.

### 3.2 Transition State
The period between STOP and re‑entry, during which the field resets rhythm, load and relational grounding.

### 3.3 Re‑Entry
The moment when the field regains coherence baseline and can resume adaptive flow.

---

## 4. When STOP Emerges
STOP emerges when the field cannot continue without harm to coherence.
These are conditions, not failures.

### 4.1 Persistent Collapse Variation
The field remains dissolved and cannot re‑form synchrony.

### 4.2 Affective Load Exceeds Regulation Capacity
The field cannot stabilize affective intensity through rhythm or framing.

### 4.3 Executive Inaccessibility Persists
The system cannot access “do something with this” functions across time.

### 4.4 Semantic Continuity Cannot Re‑Form
Meaning cannot be re‑anchored without distortion.

### 4.5 Rhythm Cannot Stabilize
The interaction rhythm remains irregular despite modulation.

### 4.6 Recurrent Shock Variation
Shock reappears immediately after softening.

### 4.7 Fragmentation Persists
Channels cannot re‑align into a coherent field.

These are signals, not judgments.

re‑establishes rhythm,

softens affective edges,

Re‑entry is not a restart.
Re‑entry is a return to synchrony.
