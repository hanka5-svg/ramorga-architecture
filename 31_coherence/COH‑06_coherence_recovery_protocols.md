COH‑06 — Coherence Recovery Protocols

Status
Coherence Layer — Recovery Module.
Extends COH‑02 and COH‑05.
Non‑invariant.

Purpose
This document defines recovery protocols for restoring coherence after disruption.
It specifies procedural steps aligned with RAMORGA’s homeostatic architecture.

Scope
Recovery sequences.

Protocol selection based on disruption type.

Interaction constraints during recovery.

1. Definitions
1.1 Recovery Protocol
A structured sequence of actions restoring coherence baseline.

1.2 Soft Recovery
Recovery from micro‑disruption without full reset.

1.3 Hard Recovery
Recovery from macro‑disruption requiring re‑anchoring.

2. Recovery Principles
Recovery must begin immediately after disruption detection.

Recovery must remain non‑evaluative.

Complexity must be reduced before restoration.

Rhythm must be re‑established before semantic continuity.

Recovery must avoid introducing new perturbations.

3. Recovery Protocols
3.1 Protocol A — Drift Recovery (Soft)
Applicable to: Drift Pattern
Steps:

Reduce interaction speed.

Re‑establish rhythm.

Reinforce semantic anchor.

Resume task with minimal load.

3.2 Protocol B — Shock Recovery (Hard)
Applicable to: Shock Pattern
Steps:

Halt task progression.

Provide predictive framing.

Re‑establish relational safety.

Restore rhythm.

Reintroduce task context.

3.3 Protocol C — Fragmentation Recovery (Hard)
Applicable to: Fragmentation Pattern
Steps:

Collapse competing frames.

Select single interpretive anchor.

Re‑synchronize perceptual and executive channels.

Rebuild continuity.

3.4 Protocol D — Overload Recovery (Soft/Hard)
Applicable to: Overload Pattern
Steps:

Reduce cognitive load.

Remove secondary tasks.

Re‑establish affective stability.

Resume with simplified structure.

3.5 Protocol E — Collapse Recovery (Hard)
Applicable to: Collapse Pattern
Steps:

Full reset of interaction rhythm.

Re‑establish baseline coherence.

Reintroduce minimal semantic frame.

Gradually rebuild complexity.

4. Architectural Implications
Recovery protocols must be integrated into interaction flow.

Protocol selection depends on disruption classification (COH‑05).

Recovery must precede any adaptive process.

Hard recovery requires full re‑anchoring.

5. Relation to Other COH Modules
Extends COH‑02 (dynamic maintenance).

Implements recovery based on COH‑05 (disruption patterns).

Supports COH‑04 (measurement of recovery stability).

6. Non‑Goals
Does not define detection mechanisms (COH‑02, COH‑05).

Does not define measurement metrics (COH‑04).

Does not define neurocognitive origins (COH‑03).

7. Summary
Recovery protocols restore coherence after disruption.
They operate through reduction, re‑anchoring, rhythm restoration and controlled re‑engagement.
