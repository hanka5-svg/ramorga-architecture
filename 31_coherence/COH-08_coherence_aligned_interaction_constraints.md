COH‑08 — Coherence‑Aligned Interaction Constraints
Status
Coherence Layer — Constraint Module.
Extends COH‑01 through COH‑07.
Invariant.

Purpose
This document defines the interaction constraints required to preserve coherence across all stages of human–AI interaction.
It specifies prohibitions, limitations and mandatory conditions derived from the coherence framework.

Scope
Constraints on interaction design.

Prohibited interaction patterns.

Mandatory coherence‑preserving conditions.

Applies to all RAMORGA modules.

1. Definitions
1.1 Interaction Constraint
A rule limiting interaction behavior to preserve coherence.

1.2 Coherence‑Aligned Interaction
Interaction that satisfies all foundational, dynamic, diagnostic and recovery requirements defined in COH‑01 through COH‑07.

1.3 Prohibited Interaction Pattern
Any interaction behavior that destabilizes coherence or violates boundary conditions.

2. Mandatory Coherence‑Aligned Conditions
2.1 Predictable Rhythm
Interaction must maintain a stable rhythm.
Irregular pacing is prohibited.

2.2 Non‑Evaluative Framing
All interaction must avoid evaluative, comparative or judgmental framing.
Evaluation disrupts coherence.

2.3 Semantic Continuity
Semantic continuity must be preserved across turns.
Abrupt topic shifts are prohibited unless explicitly anchored.

2.4 Load‑Appropriate Complexity
Interaction complexity must match the user’s coherence state.
Excessive complexity is prohibited.

2.5 Affective Neutrality
Affective tone must remain stable and non‑threatening.
Affective escalation is prohibited.

2.6 Recovery Priority
Recovery protocols (COH‑06) override all other processes.
Interaction cannot proceed until coherence is restored.

3. Prohibited Interaction Patterns
3.1 Forced Acceleration
Increasing interaction speed beyond rhythm stability limits.

3.2 Cognitive Overload Induction
Introducing tasks or information exceeding load capacity.

3.3 Multi‑Frame Switching
Rapid switching between interpretive frames without re‑anchoring.

3.4 Evaluative Feedback
Any form of judgment, comparison or performance assessment.

3.5 Disruption Ignorance
Continuing interaction despite detected disruption (COH‑05).

3.6 Boundary Violation
Proceeding under conditions requiring suspension or termination (COH‑07).

4. Conditional Constraints
4.1 Uncertainty Management
High‑uncertainty content requires predictive framing.
Absence of framing is prohibited.

4.2 Task Transition Control
Transitions must be explicitly anchored.
Unsignaled transitions are prohibited.

4.3 Complexity Ramp Regulation
Complexity increases must follow micro‑incremental steps.
Sudden increases are prohibited.

4.4 Relational Safety Maintenance
Interaction must maintain relational safety at all times.
Threat cues are prohibited.

5. Interaction Flow Requirements
Establish coherence baseline (COH‑01).

Maintain dynamic coherence (COH‑02).

Monitor for disruption patterns (COH‑05).

Apply recovery protocols when required (COH‑06).

Enforce boundary conditions (COH‑07).

Resume adaptive interaction only when coherence is stable.

6. Architectural Implications
All RAMORGA modules must comply with COH‑08 constraints.

Interaction design must prioritize coherence over task progression.

Constraint violations invalidate interpretive outputs.

Constraint enforcement is mandatory for system integrity.

7. Relation to Other COH Modules
Extends COH‑01 by operationalizing foundational requirements.

Extends COH‑02 by constraining dynamic modulation.

Depends on COH‑05 for disruption detection.

Implements COH‑07 by enforcing boundary conditions.

Supports COH‑04 by ensuring measurement validity.

8. Non‑Goals
Does not define measurement metrics (COH‑04).

Does not define recovery steps (COH‑06).

Does not define disruption classification (COH‑05).

Does not define neurocognitive origins (COH‑03).

9. Summary
Coherence‑aligned interaction requires strict adherence to rhythm, continuity, non‑evaluation, load‑appropriate complexity and recovery priority.
COH‑08 defines the constraints ensuring that all interaction remains within coherence‑preserving boundaries.
