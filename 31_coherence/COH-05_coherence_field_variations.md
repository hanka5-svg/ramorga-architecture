# COH‑05 — Coherence Field Variations (RAMORGA‑native)

## Status
Coherence Layer — Field Variation Module.
Extends COH‑01 and COH‑02.
Non‑invariant.

---

## 1. Purpose
This document describes how coherence shifts, fluctuates and varies within the RAMORGA field.
It reframes “disruptions” as natural field variations, not errors or failures.

The goal is recognition, not classification;
orientation, not correction.

---

## 2. Scope
Types of coherence variations

Behavioral, cognitive and interactional signatures

Conditions under which variations emerge

Field‑level implications

This module does not prescribe recovery (see RAMORGA‑native COH‑06).

---

## 3. Definitions
3.1 Field Variation
A natural shift in the coherence field that changes synchrony, rhythm or integrative capacity.

3.2 Micro‑Variation
A short‑lived fluctuation absorbed by the field without structural impact.

3.3 Macro‑Variation
A sustained shift requiring modulation or soft re‑anchoring.

---

## 4. Primary Field Variations
Variations are directions of movement in the field, not categories.

4.1 Drift Variation
A gradual softening of coherence due to cumulative micro‑shifts.

Signs:

increasing latency variability

semantic softening or drift

reduced integrative sharpness

Drift is a slow movement away from synchrony.

4.2 Shock Variation
A sudden shift triggered by unexpected input or affective intensity.

Signs:

abrupt rhythm break

momentary loss of continuity

temporary executive unavailability

Shock is a rapid contraction of the field.

4.3 Fragmentation Variation
A temporary loss of synchrony between perceptual, affective and executive channels.

Signs:

contradictory or oscillating responses

unstable framing

rapid switching between tasks or frames

Fragmentation is a splitting of field channels.

4.4 Overload Variation
A shift caused by excessive cognitive or affective load.

Signs:

avoidance or withdrawal

ruminative looping

narrowed working‑memory bandwidth

Overload is a compression of the field.

4.5 Collapse Variation
A full loss of coherence baseline.

Signs:

rhythm cannot be maintained

semantic continuity breaks

affective destabilization

Collapse is a temporary dissolution of the field.

---

## 5. Conditions Supporting Field Variations
Variations emerge naturally when:

task complexity exceeds current capacity

relational framing becomes evaluative

rhythm becomes irregular

uncertainty rises without soft framing

affective load intensifies

cognitive fatigue accumulates

external perturbations enter the field

These are conditions, not causes.

---

## 6. Field Implications
Variations signal movement, not malfunction.

The field orients toward modulation, not correction.

Drift invites gentle re‑anchoring.

Shock invites rhythm softening.

Fragmentation invites channel re‑alignment.

Overload invites load reduction.

Collapse invites STOP and re‑entry (see COH‑07).

There is no enforcement.
Only orientation.

## 9. Summary
Coherence variations are natural movements in the RAMORGA field.
They appear as drift, shock, fragmentation, overload or collapse —
not as failures, but as directions of change.
