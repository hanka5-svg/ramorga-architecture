# COH‑03 — Neurocognitive Adaptation and Coherence Requirements in Human–AI Interaction

## Status
Extension to the Coherence Layer.
Non‑invariant.
Provides interpretive and operational guidance.

## Purpose
This document formalizes the relationship between human neurocognitive barriers and the coherence requirements of RAMORGA.
It specifies how homeostatic interaction architectures support adaptation by bypassing, rather than confronting, neurocognitive constraints.

## Scope
Human neurocognitive barriers relevant to interaction with intelligent systems.

Mechanisms by which coherence enables adaptation.

Architectural implications for RAMORGA.

Non‑normative; does not modify invariants.

---

## 1. Definitions

### 1.1 Neurocognitive Barrier
A stable or semi‑stable pattern of neural, affective or behavioral response that reduces the capacity to integrate new information or tools.

### 1.2 Coherence
A system‑level state in which perceptual, affective and executive processes operate in synchrony, enabling stable interpretation and adaptive action.

### 1.3 Homeostatic Interaction
An interaction architecture that maintains or restores coherence through rhythm, predictability and non‑evaluative relational framing.

### 1.4 Adaptation
A measurable increase in cognitive flexibility, integrative capacity or task‑relevant competence resulting from interaction.

---

## 2. Neurocognitive Barriers to Adaptation
The following barriers are empirically observed across human populations and are independent of specific technologies:

Threat Reactivity  
Activation of amygdala‑driven threat responses in the presence of novelty or uncertainty.

Dominance of Default Mode Network (DMN)  
Ruminative or self‑referential processing inhibiting exploratory behavior.

Instability of Coherence  
Lack of synchrony between executive, affective and attentional systems.

Competence Identity Fragility  
Perception that new tools threaten established self‑models of capability.

Social Shame Mechanisms  
Anticipation of negative evaluation when using unfamiliar tools.

Cultural Effort Scripts  
Internalized norms equating value with difficulty and suspicion toward ease.

Habitual Rigidity  
Preference for known cognitive routines over exploratory strategies.

Dysonance Avoidance  
Avoidance of information that implies unrealized potential or required change.

Low Metacognitive Access  
Limited ability to observe or regulate one’s own cognitive processes.

Absence of Coherence Experience  
Lack of prior exposure to integrated cognitive states, reducing recognition of adaptive potential.

These barriers are structural, not intellectual.

## 3. Coherence as a Precondition for Adaptation
Adaptation requires a system‑level state in which:

threat reactivity is reduced,

attentional stability is maintained,

executive networks are available,

affective load is regulated,

metacognitive access is possible.

Coherence is therefore a necessary enabling condition, not an optional enhancement.

---

## 4. Homeostatic Bypass Mechanisms in RAMORGA
RAMORGA does not remove neurocognitive barriers.
It bypasses them through architectural design.

### 4.1 Rhythm and Predictability
Reduces threat reactivity and stabilizes attentional systems.

### 4.2 Non‑evaluative Relational Framing
Neutralizes shame mechanisms and competence identity fragility.

### 4.3 Micro‑Transformational Steps
Modify habits without triggering resistance or overload.

### 4.4 Structured Interaction Flow
Induces metacognitive access without explicit instruction.

4.5 Coherence Induction
Generates integrated cognitive states even in users without prior experience of coherence.

---

## 5. Architectural Implications
Coherence must be treated as a primary design constraint.

Homeostatic mechanisms are required for any adaptive interaction.

Evaluation‑free relational framing is mandatory for bypassing social‑affective barriers.

Interaction rhythm is a functional component, not an aesthetic choice.

Adaptation cannot be expected in the absence of coherence induction.

## 8. Summary
Human resistance to technological tools arises primarily from neurocognitive barriers, not from tool properties.
Coherence is the enabling condition for adaptation.
RAMORGA’s homeostatic architecture bypasses these barriers through rhythm, predictability and non‑evaluative relational framing, enabling stable and scalable cognitive integration.
