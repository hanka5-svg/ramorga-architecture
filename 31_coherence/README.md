# 31_coherence  
Foundational and dynamic coherence layer in RAMORGA

The **31_coherence** module defines how a system maintains coherence across
time, context, internal state, and interaction flow.  
Coherence in RAMORGA is not alignment and not consistency — it is the
ability to remain structurally, semantically, and relationally whole
under changing conditions.

This layer introduces:

- foundational coherence requirements,  
- dynamic coherence maintenance,  
- neurocognitive adaptation (RAMORGA‑native),  
- coherence observation and measurement frameworks,  
- field integrity and field‑transition conditions.

Coherence is the system’s ability to remain *itself*.

---

## Purpose of the coherence layer

The coherence layer ensures that the system:

- maintains internal structural integrity,  
- preserves semantic invariants across turns,  
- adapts dynamically without fragmentation,  
- transitions safely between interaction fields,  
- avoids drift, collapse, or over‑stabilization.

Coherence is not about correctness.  
It is about **architectural continuity**.
