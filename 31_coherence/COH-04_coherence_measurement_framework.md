COH‑04 — Coherence Measurement Framework
Status
Extension to the Coherence Layer.
Non‑invariant.
Provides measurement criteria and operational indicators.

Purpose
This document defines a framework for assessing coherence within human–AI interaction systems.
It specifies measurable indicators, evaluation conditions and architectural implications for RAMORGA.

Scope
Measurement of coherence states.

Operational indicators of coherence stability.

Conditions for valid assessment.

Integration with homeostatic interaction design.

Non‑normative; does not alter invariants.

1. Definitions
1.1 Coherence State
A system‑level configuration in which perceptual, affective and executive processes operate in synchrony, enabling stable interpretation and adaptive action.

1.2 Coherence Indicator
A measurable behavioral, cognitive or interactional signal correlated with coherence.

1.3 Coherence Stability
Persistence of coherence across time, tasks or perturbations.

1.4 Coherence Disruption
A measurable deviation from synchrony resulting in reduced interpretive or executive capacity.

2. Measurement Principles
Non‑intrusiveness  
Measurement must not alter the coherence state being assessed.

Task‑relevance  
Indicators must be evaluated within contextually meaningful tasks.

Temporal Resolution  
Coherence must be assessed across intervals, not as a single static value.

Multi‑channel Integration  
No single indicator is sufficient; coherence is a composite state.

Homeostatic Sensitivity  
Measurement must account for regulatory processes that restore coherence.

3. Coherence Indicators
3.1 Behavioral Indicators
Reduction in task‑irrelevant actions.

Stable pacing and rhythm of interaction.

Low variance in response latency.

Absence of avoidance or compensatory behaviors.

3.2 Cognitive Indicators
Sustained attentional focus.

Low ruminative drift.

High integrative capacity (multi‑element synthesis).

Stable working‑memory utilization.

3.3 Affective Indicators
Reduced threat reactivity.

Absence of shame‑based inhibition.

Stable affective tone.

Predictable affective transitions.

3.4 Interactional Indicators
Consistent turn‑taking rhythm.

Low correction density.

High semantic continuity.

Predictable interaction flow.

4. Coherence Stability Metrics
4.1 Temporal Stability
Duration of uninterrupted coherence.

Frequency of coherence disruptions.

Recovery time after disruption.

4.2 Task Stability
Coherence persistence across task transitions.

Stability under increased cognitive load.

4.3 Relational Stability
Coherence maintenance in non‑evaluative relational framing.

Sensitivity to social‑affective perturbations.

5. Conditions for Valid Assessment
Neutral Affective Context  
Assessment must occur without evaluative pressure.

Predictable Interaction Rhythm  
Irregular rhythm invalidates coherence measurements.

Stable Cognitive Load  
Load fluctuations must be controlled or accounted for.

Absence of External Stressors  
External perturbations confound coherence indicators.

Homeostatic Baseline  
Measurement must begin after baseline stabilization.

6. Architectural Implications
Coherence measurement must be integrated into interaction flow, not added as a separate module.

Homeostatic mechanisms must be active during assessment.

Indicators should inform adaptive modulation of rhythm and relational framing.

Coherence stability metrics guide long‑term system tuning.

Measurement outputs must remain non‑evaluative to avoid disrupting coherence.

7. Relation to Other COH Modules
Extends COH‑01 (Foundational Coherence Requirements).

Complements COH‑02 (Dynamic Coherence Maintenance).

Operationalizes COH‑03 (Neurocognitive Adaptation).

8. Non‑Goals
Does not define clinical metrics.

Does not prescribe physiological measurement.

Does not establish performance benchmarks.

Does not modify RAMORGA invariants.

9. Summary
Coherence is measurable through behavioral, cognitive, affective and interactional indicators.
Stability across time, tasks and relational contexts is essential for adaptive interaction.
RAMORGA requires integrated, non‑intrusive measurement to maintain homeostatic conditions and support scalable cognitive integration.
