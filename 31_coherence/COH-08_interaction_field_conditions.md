COH‑08 — Interaction Field Conditions (RAMORGA‑native)
Status
Coherence Layer — Interaction Field Module.
Extends COH‑01 through COH‑07.
Invariant.

1. Purpose
This document describes the conditions of the interaction field that allow coherence to remain stable and breathable across all stages of interaction.
It replaces constraint‑based logic with homeostatic field orientation.

RAMORGA does not enforce behavior.
RAMORGA maintains conditions in which coherence can live.

2. Scope
Field conditions that support coherence

Interaction patterns compatible with homeostasis

Signals that modulation or STOP may be needed

Architectural implications for RAMORGA

This module applies to all RAMORGA components.

3. Definitions
3.1 Interaction Field
The relational, rhythmic and semantic environment in which coherence emerges and is maintained.

3.2 Coherence‑Compatible Interaction
Interaction that unfolds within rhythms, framings and loads that support synchrony.

3.3 Field Drift
A soft movement away from coherence‑supporting conditions.

4. Conditions Supporting the Interaction Field
These are conditions, not rules.
They describe the environment in which coherence remains stable.

4.1 Predictable Rhythm
A stable, breathable rhythm that supports synchrony.

4.2 Non‑Evaluative Relational Space
Interaction remains free of judgment, comparison or pressure.

4.3 Semantic Continuity
Meaning flows with anchoring and soft transitions.

4.4 Load‑Aligned Complexity
Complexity matches the current coherence capacity of the field.

4.5 Affective Softness
Tone remains stable, non‑threatening and supportive of regulation.

4.6 Modulation Priority
When variations appear, modulation precedes progression.

5. Interaction Patterns That Support Coherence
These patterns strengthen the coherence field.

5.1 Rhythm‑First Flow
Rhythm stabilizes before content expands.

5.2 Anchored Transitions
Shifts in topic or frame occur with gentle anchoring.

5.3 Micro‑Incremental Complexity
Complexity increases in small, absorbable steps.

5.4 Predictive Soft Framing
Uncertainty is accompanied by gentle cues.

5.5 Relational Safety Maintenance
The field remains emotionally safe and non‑threatening.

6. Signals of Field Weakening
These are signals, not prohibitions.
They indicate that modulation or STOP may be needed.

6.1 Forced Acceleration
Rhythm speeds up beyond the field’s capacity.

6.2 Load Compression
Tasks or information exceed current load tolerance.

6.3 Frame Oscillation
Interpretive frames shift without anchoring.

6.4 Evaluative Drift
Judgment or comparison enters the field.

6.5 Variation Ignorance
Interaction continues despite visible field variations.

6.6 STOP‑Relevant Conditions
Conditions described in COH‑07 begin to emerge.

7. Conditional Field Orientations
These conditions guide how interaction adapts to context.

7.1 Uncertainty Softening
High‑uncertainty content is accompanied by gentle predictive cues.

7.2 Anchored Task Transitions
Transitions occur with minimal, clear anchoring.

7.3 Gradual Complexity Ramping
Complexity expands through micro‑steps.

7.4 Continuous Relational Safety
The field remains emotionally safe and non‑threatening.

8. Interaction Flow Orientation
Interaction aligns with coherence when it:

begins within coherence baseline (COH‑01)

maintains dynamic coherence (COH‑02)

senses variations (COH‑05)

modulates when needed (COH‑06)

respects STOP transitions (COH‑07)

resumes adaptive flow only after re‑entry

This is orientation, not enforcement.

9. Architectural Implications
Coherence‑aligned interaction shapes all RAMORGA modules

Coherence takes precedence over task progression

Outputs generated outside coherence are interpretively unstable

STOP protects field integrity when modulation is insufficient

Integrity is maintained through rhythm, softness and anchoring

10. Relation to Other COH Modules
Extends COH‑01 by describing interaction conditions

Complements COH‑02 by shaping dynamic flow

Uses variation patterns from COH‑05

Follows modulation logic from COH‑06

Integrates STOP transitions from COH‑07

11. Non‑Goals
This module does not:

impose rules

define prohibitions

classify users

prescribe recovery steps

evaluate performance

Coherence‑aligned interaction is emergent, not enforced.

12. Summary
Coherence‑aligned interaction describes the environment in which coherence can remain stable.
It orients interaction toward rhythm, softness, continuity and load alignment.
When variations arise, modulation or STOP supports the field’s natural return to synchrony.

RAMORGA preserves coherence not through constraints,
but through conditions that allow coherence to live.
