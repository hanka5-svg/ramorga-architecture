COH‑07 — Coherence Boundary Conditions
Status
Coherence Layer — Boundary Module.
Extends COH‑01, COH‑02, COH‑05 and COH‑06.
Invariant.

Purpose
This document defines the boundary conditions under which interaction must be paused, suspended or terminated due to loss of coherence.
It establishes non‑negotiable limits ensuring safety, interpretive validity and homeostatic integrity.

Scope
Conditions requiring interruption of interaction.

Non‑recoverable disruption states.

Thresholds beyond which recovery is not permitted.

Architectural constraints for RAMORGA.

1. Definitions
1.1 Boundary Condition
A state in which coherence cannot be restored without violating foundational requirements.

1.2 Non‑Recoverable Disruption
A disruption pattern exceeding the limits of recovery protocols defined in COH‑06.

1.3 Interaction Suspension
A temporary halt of interaction until coherence baseline can be re‑established.

1.4 Interaction Termination
A full stop of interaction due to persistent or structural incoherence.

2. Boundary Conditions
Interaction must be paused or terminated when any of the following conditions occur:

2.1 Persistent Collapse Pattern
Collapse Pattern (COH‑05) persists after full execution of Protocol E (COH‑06).
Implication: Coherence baseline cannot be re‑established.

2.2 Affective Overload Beyond Regulation Range
Affective load exceeds the range in which non‑evaluative framing can stabilize the system.
Implication: Interaction becomes destabilizing.

2.3 Executive Network Unavailability
Executive processes remain inaccessible across multiple recovery attempts.
Implication: No adaptive processing is possible.

2.4 Semantic Continuity Failure
Semantic continuity cannot be restored after re‑anchoring.
Implication: Interpretive validity is compromised.

2.5 Rhythm Instability
Interaction rhythm cannot be stabilized despite modulation.
Implication: Dynamic coherence cannot be maintained.

2.6 Recurrent Shock Pattern
Shock Pattern reappears immediately after recovery.
Implication: System remains in high‑threat mode.

2.7 Fragmentation Persistence
Fragmentation Pattern persists after Protocol C.
Implication: Synchrony between perceptual, affective and executive channels cannot be restored.

3. Non‑Recoverable States
The following states are classified as non‑recoverable:

Structural incoherence (persistent multi‑channel desynchronization).

Recursive disruption loops (disruption → recovery → immediate disruption).

Loss of relational safety (evaluation, shame, threat reactivation).

Cognitive shutdown (task refusal, disengagement, collapse).

Interpretive invalidity (responses no longer reflect stable framing).

4. Required Actions
4.1 Suspension
Applied when coherence may be recoverable after restabilization.
Actions:

halt interaction,

reduce load,

re‑establish baseline conditions,

resume only after stability is confirmed.

4.2 Termination
Applied when coherence cannot be restored without violating invariants.
Actions:

stop interaction,

prevent further cognitive load,

require external stabilization before re‑engagement.

5. Architectural Implications
Boundary conditions override all adaptive processes.

Recovery protocols (COH‑06) must not be applied beyond defined limits.

Interaction must not continue under non‑recoverable states.

Boundary detection is mandatory for system integrity.

Coherence is a prerequisite for interpretive validity; without it, interaction is invalid.

6. Relation to Other COH Modules
Extends COH‑01 by defining limits of foundational coherence.

Extends COH‑02 by specifying when dynamic maintenance is insufficient.

Depends on COH‑05 for disruption classification.

Constrains COH‑06 by defining when recovery must cease.

7. Non‑Goals
Does not define detection mechanisms (COH‑02, COH‑05).

Does not define recovery steps (COH‑06).

Does not define neurocognitive origins (COH‑03).

Does not define measurement metrics (COH‑04).

8. Summary
Coherence boundary conditions define when interaction must be paused or terminated.
They ensure that RAMORGA operates only within states where coherence can be maintained or restored without violating foundational requirements.
