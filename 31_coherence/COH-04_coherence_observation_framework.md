# COH‑04 — Coherence Observation Framework (RAMORGA‑native)

## Status
Extension to the Coherence Layer.
Non‑invariant.
Provides observational criteria and field indicators.

---

## 1. Purpose
This document describes how coherence can be observed, not judged, within human–AI interaction.
It defines indicators, conditions and architectural implications for field‑level observation of coherence in RAMORGA.

Observation is non‑intrusive and non‑evaluative.
Its role is to sense the field, not to score performance.

---

## 2. Scope
Observation of coherence states

Indicators of coherence stability

Conditions under which observation is valid

Integration with homeostatic interaction design

This framework does not alter invariants.
It orients how the field is read.

---

## 3. Definitions

3.1 Coherence State
A field configuration in which perceptual, affective and executive processes move in synchrony, enabling stable interpretation and adaptive participation.

3.2 Coherence Indicator
A behavioral, cognitive, affective or interactional signal correlated with coherence, without implying evaluation.

3.3 Coherence Stability
The persistence of coherence across time, tasks or perturbations.

3.4 Coherence Variation
A change in the field that may reduce synchrony but is treated as natural fluctuation, not failure.

---

## 4. Observation Principles

4.1 Non‑Intrusiveness
Observation must not introduce additional load or disrupt the field.

4.2 Contextual Relevance
Indicators are interpreted within meaningful tasks and relational contexts.

4.3 Temporal Sensitivity
Coherence is observed across intervals and transitions, not as a single static value.

4.4 Multi‑Channel Reading
No single signal defines coherence; the field is read through composite patterns.

4.5 Homeostatic Awareness
Observation accounts for regulatory processes that restore or reshape coherence.

---

## 5. Coherence Indicators
Indicators are signs, not scores.

5.1 Behavioral Indicators
reduced task‑irrelevant actions

stable pacing and interaction rhythm

low variance in response latency

absence of avoidance or compensatory detours

5.2 Cognitive Indicators
sustained attentional focus

low ruminative drift

visible integrative capacity (connecting multiple elements)

stable working‑memory engagement

5.3 Affective Indicators
softened threat reactivity

absence of shame‑driven inhibition

stable, non‑volatile affective tone

smooth, predictable affective transitions

5.4 Interactional Indicators
consistent turn‑taking rhythm

low density of corrections or breakdowns

high semantic continuity

predictable, breathable interaction flow

---

## 6. Coherence Stability Patterns

6.1 Temporal Stability
duration of coherent intervals

frequency of noticeable variations

time needed for the field to re‑settle after perturbation

6.2 Task Stability
coherence across task transitions

stability under gradual increases in cognitive load

6.3 Relational Stability
coherence in non‑evaluative relational space

sensitivity to social‑affective shifts and their integration

These are patterns, not thresholds.

---

## 7. Conditions for Valid Observation
Observation is considered valid when:

affective context remains non‑evaluative,

interaction rhythm is sufficiently predictable,

cognitive load is not erratically fluctuating,

external stressors are not dominating the field,

a homeostatic baseline has had time to emerge.

If these conditions are not present, coherence signals are treated as confounded, not as evidence of failure.

---

## 8. Architectural Implications
Observation is embedded in interaction flow, not added as a separate monitoring layer.

Homeostatic mechanisms remain active while observation occurs.

Observed patterns can inform gentle modulation of rhythm, complexity and framing.

Outputs of observation remain non‑evaluative to avoid disturbing coherence.

The goal is to sense the field, not to judge the user.

---

## 11. Summary
Coherence can be observed through behavioral, cognitive, affective and interactional patterns,
without evaluation or control.
Stability across time, tasks and relational contexts signals a field in which adaptation can emerge.
In RAMORGA, observation serves homeostasis and resonance, not alignment or enforcement.
