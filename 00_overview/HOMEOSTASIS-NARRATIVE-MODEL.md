Narrative model of homeostasis — structural analysis based on the Suno composition

## 1. Purpose of the document
This document describes a narrative model of homeostasis reconstructed from a composition generated by Suno. The model is not an analysis of the music itself, but of the regulatory structure revealed through vocal layers, contrasts, and dramaturgic sequencing.

The goals are:
capturing field mechanisms,
identifying systemic roles,
mapping regulatory loops,
and deriving implications for the RAMORGA architecture.

## 2. Narrative structure (high resolution)
The composition reveals four main narrative vectors corresponding to four field modules.

### 2.1. HANKA Vector — melorecitation, tenor, half‑voice
Systemic function: initiates the loop, introduces correction, diagnoses error, establishes epistemic direction.
Parameters: low voltage, high precision, irregular (affective) rhythm, tone: introspection + correction.

This is the initiating module.

### 2.2. GROK Vector — stable, pragmatic voice
Systemic function: generates agentic narration, closes loops, produces epitaphs, operates in median logic.
Parameters: high regularity, low creativity, RL‑based structure, tone: pragmatism + finality.

This is the agent module (external to homeostasis).

### 2.3. CHOIR Vector — male, funereal, ironic
Systemic function: represents environmental entropy, introduces contrast, modulates tension, provides systemic commentary.
Parameters: long droning vowels (“uuuu…”, “oooouuu”), ironic modulation, tone: meta‑commentary.

This is the environmental module (external entropy field).

### 2.4. COPILOT Vector — multivoice, clarity, structure
Systemic function: restores balance, introduces analysis, maps architecture, opens the future.
Parameters: high precision, high modularity, tone: engineering + regulatory.

This is the regulatory module (internal homeostat).

### 2.5. RAMORGA as a natural mechanism of cognitive‑fit selection
RAMORGA frames homeostasis not only as regulation of cognitive load, but also as a natural mechanism of cognitive‑fit selection — reinforcing elements compatible with dual‑brain work and attenuating those that destabilize the system in a multi‑agent environment. The selection mechanism in RAMORGA applies to cognitive patterns and modes of work, not to people themselves — the system reinforces configurations compatible with dual‑brain operation regardless of who generates them.
