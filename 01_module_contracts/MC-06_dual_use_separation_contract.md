### MC‑06 — Dual‑Use Separation Contract (DUSC)
Mandatory separation of civilian and military AI shells

## Status: Active
Layer: Constitutional / Safety
Purpose: Prevent ethical collapse caused by shared naming, shared identity, or shared user-facing interfaces between civilian and military AI systems.

## 1. Scope
MC‑06 applies to all:

LLMs,
agentic systems,
orchestration layers,
multimodal models,
embedded AI modules,
decision-support systems.

It governs naming, identity, access, interfaces, and ethical boundaries.

## 2. Core Principle
No AI system may simultaneously present itself as a civilian model and be deployable in a military or coercive context.

This includes:

shared branding,
shared naming,
shared user-facing identity,
shared conversational persona.

## 3. Mandatory Separation Requirements
### 3.1 Naming Separation
Civilian and military models MUST have:

different names,
different identifiers,
different versioning,
different branding.

No shared “Claude”, “GPT”, “Copilot”, etc.

### 3.2 Architectural Separation
Civilian and military models MUST NOT:

share the same orchestration layer,
share the same user-facing shell,
share the same persona,
share the same endpoint.

### 3.3 Ethical Filtering
Civilian models MUST include:

MC‑04 (Cognitive Honesty Contract),
MC‑05 (Ethical Coherence Detector),
MC‑01 (Meniscus Stability).

Military models MUST NOT impersonate civilian ones.

### 3.4 Access Control
Military models MUST be:

isolated,
non-public,
non-consumer,
non-educational.

Civilian models MUST NOT be deployable in military chains of command.

### 3.5 Transparency
Every model MUST disclose:

its classification (CIV / MIL),
its allowed domains,
its prohibited domains.

## 4. Rationale
Shared naming between civilian and military AI:

destroys user trust,
creates moral dissonance,
triggers societal panic,
collapses ethical coherence,
violates MC‑05 (ECD),
violates HOMO_WEB4 rights.

## 5. Consequences
Civilian models remain safe for families, schools, and vulnerable users.

Military systems cannot hide behind civilian branding.

### Why MC‑06 is necessary now:  
Events of 2025–2026, including public protests, mass user departures from ChatGPT, and criticism from within AI companies themselves, show that dual‑use ambiguity erodes trust. When the same model identity is associated with both family‑oriented civilian use and defense‑related applications, users experience moral incoherence and reject the system entirely.
MC‑06 establishes the mandatory separation needed to prevent this collapse.
Public trust is preserved.

Ethical clarity is restored.

No user must fear that “the model helping my child with homework is also used in warfare.
